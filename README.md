# Aviation Accident Analysis


This project analyzes aviation accident data to evaluate the safety performance of different aircraft makes and models. The analysis focuses on identifying aircraft with lower rates of destruction and lower probabilities of fatal or serious passenger injuries during accidents.

## Objectives

* Explore trends in aviation accident data
* Compare safety metrics across aircraft manufacturers and plane types
* Separate aircraft into small and large categories using a passenger threshold
* Evaluate:

  * Fatal and serious injury rates
  * Aircraft destruction rates
* Visualize safety distributions using plots such as violin plots and strip plots
* Provide recommendations on safer aircraft makes and models based on the findings

## Tools & Libraries

* Python
* Pandas
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn

## Key Insights

* Large aircraft generally showed lower destruction rates across manufacturers.
* Certain aircraft makes consistently demonstrated lower injury risk profiles.
* Visualizations highlighted the distribution and variability of injury rates among aircraft categories.

## Outcome

The project provides data-driven insights that can support airline companies or aviation insurers in identifying safer aircraft options and understanding factors associated with aviation accident severity.
